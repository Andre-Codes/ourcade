# High Card Bust — How to Play

*Rules for the **Lane-Timer edition** — the version where every open lane runs a
resolution countdown. If your build doesn't show a ⏱ timer above each lane, you're
on an older ruleset and these rules won't all match.*

---

## The idea

You're playing poker against the clock, by yourself. Cards come to you one at a
time. You drop each one into one of your **lanes**, trying to build a good five-card
poker hand in each. Strong hands pay you **chips** and rack up **points**. Weak
hands pay little — and a dead lane can lock up for good.

You're juggling **three lanes at once**, and here's the twist: **every open lane is
on a timer.** Ignore a lane too long and it resolves on its own, with whatever cards
happen to be sitting in it. So you can't just park cards and wait for perfect hands —
you have to commit.

Your goal is simple: **score as many points as you can before the board falls
apart.** The run ends when all your lanes are locked, or you're out of chips with
nowhere legal to play.

---

## What you're working with

- **3 lanes.** Each holds up to **5 cards** (one poker hand). Lanes start closed.
- **12 starting chips.** Chips are your fuel — you spend them to open lanes and to
  raise bets, and you earn them back by making hands. Run out and you can't open new
  lanes.
- **1 discard.** A single "get out of this card" token (more on it below).
- **2 save tokens.** Emergency insurance against a bad lane (explained under
  *Resolving a lane*).

---

## Your turn, step by step

1. **A card is drawn** into your tray (the single card shown near the top).
2. **You place it** into one of your lanes — tap the lane once to greenlight it, tap
   again to drop the card. (Or spend your discard to throw the card away instead.)
3. A **fresh card is drawn**, and it's your turn again.

Every card you draw ticks down the timers on the lanes you *didn't* just feed. That's
the pressure — keep reading.

---

## Opening a lane and the ANTE

Lanes start empty and closed. **To start using a lane, you pay its ANTE** — this
happens automatically the first time you drop a card into an empty lane.

- The ante starts at **1 chip**.
- **The ante rises as the run goes on** (like blinds climbing in real poker). It goes
  up by **1 chip** for every:
  - **5 scoring hands** you make,
  - **2 Wanted objectives** you complete, and
  - **20 cards** you draw.
  These stack, and the ante **never goes back down.** Late in a run, opening a fresh
  lane can cost several chips — so opening lanes gets more expensive the longer you
  survive.

You'll see `ANTE 1` (or `NEED 3`, etc.) on an empty lane. If you can't afford the
ante, you can't open that lane.

> **A lane you've already opened is always free to keep playing** — the ante is a
> one-time cost to *open* it. You only need chips on hand to open a *new* lane or to
> *raise*.

---

## The lane timer ⏱ — the heart of this version

Every **open** lane shows a **countdown** (starting at **10**). This is the number of
draws that lane has left before it **forces itself to resolve**.

- **Placing a card into a lane refreshes its timer** back to 10. A lane you keep
  working on stays alive.
- **Every draw ticks down all the *other* open lanes.** The lanes you're ignoring are
  quietly running out of time.
- When a lane's timer hits **0**, it **resolves right then** — with **whatever cards
  are in it**, even if it only has two or three. A rushed lane usually makes a weak
  hand… or nothing at all, which busts it.

The timer even ticks while you **discard** — a discard is still a draw, so ignored
lanes keep aging. The countdown turns **red and pulses** when a lane is about to go.

**The skill of this version:** don't open more lanes than you can feed. Keep a couple
going and focus on finishing the one that's about to time out. Spreading yourself thin
across all three lanes is how you end up force-resolving garbage.

---

## Resolving a lane — the three outcomes

A lane resolves either **when you fill it to 5 cards** or **when its timer runs out**.
Either way, the cards in it are read as a poker hand, and one of three things happens:

### ✅ SCORE — Two Pair or better
The hand scores points, the lane **clears** (freeing the slot to reopen), your
**discard refreshes**, and you're **paid chips** (see *Earning chips* below). This is
what you want.

### 🛟 SAVE — exactly a Pair
A single pair doesn't score, but it's not a disaster **if you have a save token**:
spend one, and the lane clears for 0 points (you lose the ante, but the lane isn't
lost). **With no save tokens left, a pair busts and locks** just like a high card.
- You start with **2 save tokens** and earn **+1 every 5th scoring hand** (up to a max
  of **4**). Watch the **SAVES** count near your chips.

### 💥 BUST — High Card (nothing made)
No pair, no better — the lane **locks permanently.** You lose the ante (and any raise
on it), your Wanted **streak resets**, and that lane is gone for the rest of the run.
This is the pressure the timer creates: a lane you were forced to resolve early often
comes up empty-handed and busts.

> **Locking all three lanes ends the run.** Every locked lane is one fewer place to
> play — the board tightens as you go.

---

## Earning chips

Chips come back to you **only when a lane SCORES** (Two Pair or better). Here's the
math:

**You get your ante back, plus a profit that scales with how good the hand is:**

| Hand           | Profit (chips **on top of** your returned ante) |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Two Pair       | +1                                              |
| Three of a Kind| +2                                              |
| Straight       | +3                                              |
| Flush          | +4                                              |
| Full House     | +6                                              |
| Four of a Kind | +10                                             |
| Straight Flush | +14                                             |
| Royal Flush    | +20                                             |

So a scoring lane **always at least breaks even** (ante back + a little profit), and
**big hands pay real money.** A rushed two-pair keeps you afloat; a full house
actually grows your bank. This is why chasing stronger hands matters — not just for
points, but for the chips that let you keep opening lanes as the ante climbs.

**A bust or a save returns nothing** — you lose the ante you paid (and any raise).

> The number shown floating over an open lane is the chips you'd pocket if it scores.
> Before the lane is finished the game can't know your final hand, so it shows the
> **floor** (the two-pair payout). Land something bigger and you'll be paid more.

---

## Raising — betting for a multiplier

On top of the ante, you can **raise** a lane to wager more chips for a **points
multiplier** and extra chip profit. Tap the **chip button** under a lane to cycle
through the raise tiers.

**When you can raise:** the lane must be **open**, **unlocked**, and hold **3 cards or
fewer** with **no made hand yet** (you can't raise a lane that already has a pair or
better — you have to call your shot early).

**The tiers:**

| Raise  | Extra cost | Needs to win  | Points multiplier | Chips back if it wins |
| ------ | ---------- | ------------- | ----------------- | --------------------- |
| Red    | 1 chip     | Trips+        | ×3                | your 1 back + 1 profit |
| Gold   | 2 chips    | Straight+     | ×5                | your 2 back + 2 profit |
| Black  | 4 chips    | Full House+   | ×8                | your 4 back + 3 profit |

**How a raise plays out:**
- You **cycle** the chip to preview a tier — nothing is spent yet. The raise
  **commits on your next draw** (that's when the chips are actually deducted).
- Once committed, the raise **must land within 5 draws** or it **expires** (the stake
  is gone; the lane itself keeps playing normally). A small countdown on the chip
  shows the draws left.
- **If the lane resolves at or above the raise's requirement**, you win: the hand's
  points are **multiplied**, and you get your raise stake back **plus** its profit —
  on top of the normal ante payout.
- **If the lane scores but falls short** of the raise (e.g. you raised Gold needing a
  Straight but only made Two Pair), the hand **still scores normally** — you just
  **lose the raised chips**, no multiplier.
- **If the lane busts or saves,** you lose everything staked (ante and raise).

So the payout number over a raised lane is the **total** you'd collect on a win: ante
back + ante profit + raise back + raise profit, with the points multiplier applied to
the hand.

---

## The WANTED objective

A rotating **WANTED** target is shown up top. Complete it **on a scoring lane** for
bonus points and chips, and to build a **streak**. Tap the WANTED bar in-game for the
current target and exactly how to complete it.

- A Wanted is either a **specific hand** (make *exactly* that hand — e.g. "Two Pair")
  or a **condition** on the five cards (e.g. "All Red," "Blackjack 21," "Rainbow
  Lane"). A condition still requires the lane to **score** (Two Pair+) as well.
- Completing a Wanted pays a **bonus** (bigger for harder targets) and advances your
  **streak**. The targets get harder as your streak climbs.

**Streak milestones** (based on the streak you reach):
- **2 →** +25% bonus points
- **3 →** +1 bonus chip
- **4 →** +50% bonus points
- **5 →** **unlock one locked lane** (a second chance!)

**A bust resets your streak to zero.** Every completed Wanted also nudges the ante up.

---

## The JACKPOT

Land a **Straight Flush** or a **Royal Flush** in any lane, any time, and you hit the
**JACKPOT** — a huge bonus on top of the normal payout, regardless of the current
Wanted:

- **Straight Flush →** +1000 points, +8 chips
- **Royal Flush →** +2500 points, +10 chips

These also count toward your streak like a Wanted.

---

## The discard

You have **one discard.** Instead of placing the drawn card, you can throw it away —
handy when the card would wreck a lane.

- Using the discard **spends it.** It **recharges when any lane scores** (Two Pair+).
- **Discarding still counts as a draw** — so your open lanes' timers still tick while
  you discard. It buys you out of a bad card, not out of the clock.

---

## Scoring & the end of the run

Your **score** is the sum of every hand's base points (× any winning raise
multiplier), plus all your Wanted and Jackpot bonuses. It's what goes on the arcade
leaderboard.

The run **ends** when:
- **all three lanes are locked**, or
- your drawn card has **no legal lane** to go into (you're out of chips and out of
  room).

Then your final score is locked in.

---

## Quick strategy tips

- **Don't over-open.** Three lanes all ticking at once is more than you can feed.
  Keep one or two lanes as your focus and finish them before their timers bite.
- **Watch the red timer.** When a lane goes urgent, decide fast: can a card here still
  make it at least Two Pair? If not, it's going to bust — cut your losses and feed the
  lane you *can* save.
- **Chase strength when you can.** Bigger hands pay far more chips (and points), and
  chips are what let you keep opening lanes as the ante rises.
- **Raise early and honestly.** You can only raise a lane before it makes a hand, so
  raise the lanes you genuinely believe in — and remember the 5-draw clock.
- **Spend save tokens wisely.** They're your only defense against a token-less pair
  busting a lane; you only get a few, so save them for lanes you can't rescue any
  other way.
